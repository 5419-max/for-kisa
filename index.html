Two Sunflowers

They say sunflowers always follow the sun. That's the part most people know. But there's something they don't tell you — when the night comes and the sun is gone, they don't just go still. They turn toward each other.

That's the part that got me.

Because I've been thinking about what that means for us. The world will have its bright days — the kind where everything feels easy and nothing needs to be said. But it'll also have its 2am seasons. The ones where the sky doesn't cooperate and life feels heavier than it should.

And in those moments, I don't want to be someone who only shows up in the sunshine. I want to be the thing you turn to when it's dark. Your warmth when the world goes cold. Your stillness when everything else is loud.

I can't hand you sunflowers right now — they're not exactly lining the streets of Kampala. But I built you this instead. Because you deserve something made, not just bought.

Two sunflowers. Turning toward each other. Even when the night is long.

That's what I want for us, Kisa.

— Ian
